# Platform Mission

## Mission Statement

> **Democratise trusted collaboration for humans and AI by making identity the universal foundation of every interaction.**

---

## Why Identity?

Every meaningful interaction — a conversation, a transaction, a creative act — requires that participants can answer three questions:

1. **Who are you?** — authentication and attestation.
2. **What can you do?** — authorisation and delegation.
3. **Why are you here?** — intent, context, and accountability.

Existing platforms answer these questions partially, inconsistently, or not at all for AI agents. The Identity Operating System answers all three, for humans and agents alike, in a composable and auditable way.

---

## Strategic Pillars

### 1. Universal Identity
Every participant — person, organisation, AI agent, or autonomous process — is represented by a richly typed `Identity` object. Identity is portable, verifiable, and self-sovereign.

### 2. Contextual Trust
Trust is not a single yes/no gate. It is contextual: what level of trust is warranted for *this action*, *at this time*, *in this context*? The platform provides fine-grained, time-bounded trust assertions that expire, escalate, and can be audited.

### 3. Agentic Collaboration
AI agents carry their own identity, operate within delegated authority, and can collaborate with humans and other agents transparently. Agency is not a loophole in the identity model — it is a first-class feature.

### 4. Auditability by Default
Every action is logged against the identity that performed it. There are no anonymous operations. Audit trails are tamper-evident and exportable.

### 5. Open Ecosystem
The platform is open-source, protocol-first, and designed for composability. Organisations can run it on their own infrastructure or use hosted services. There is no vendor lock-in.

---

## Success Looks Like

- An individual developer can bootstrap a trusted AI collaboration session in under five minutes.
- An enterprise can replace its bespoke IAM, delegation, and audit systems with the Identity OS and reduce complexity while improving security posture.
- AI agents built on the platform are fully traceable — their actions, decisions, and delegated authorities are all linked to their verified identity.
- The open-source community extends the platform with new identity providers, agent runtimes, and collaboration protocols.

---

## What We Are Not

- We are **not** a general-purpose identity provider (IdP). We build *on top of* IdPs and federate with them.
- We are **not** a model vendor. We are AI-model-agnostic; agents can be powered by any model.
- We are **not** a communication platform. We provide the identity and trust layer; communication protocols are pluggable.
