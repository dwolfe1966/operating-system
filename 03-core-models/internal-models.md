# Internal Models

> "People do not act on reality.
> They act on their internal model of reality."

---

# Purpose

This document defines our current model of Internal Models.

Internal Models are the mechanism through which people understand the world.

Representations do not directly change decisions.

Representations change internal models.

Improved internal models produce better understanding.

Better understanding enables better decisions.

---

# Core Question

How do people construct and update their understanding of reality?

---

# Working Model

Every person maintains internal models of the world.

These models are continually updated through observation, experience, memory, communication, and reasoning.

Products do not change reality.

Products influence the user's internal model of reality.

The purpose of an Identity Intelligence Platform is to improve the user's internal model of another person's identity.

---

# Why This Model Exists

Reality is inaccessible in its entirety.

Humans therefore construct internal representations that approximate reality.

These internal models allow people to:

- predict
- reason
- explain
- remember
- decide
- act

Without internal models, understanding cannot exist.

---

# Internal Models Are Always Incomplete

No internal model is complete.

Every person operates with:

- missing information
- outdated information
- incorrect assumptions
- uncertainty
- bias

The purpose of software is not to eliminate these limitations.

It is to improve the quality of the user's model.

---

# Internal Models Continuously Evolve

Internal models change whenever:

- new evidence appears
- existing beliefs are challenged
- confidence changes
- relationships become clearer
- patterns emerge
- explanations improve

Understanding is therefore continuous rather than static.

---

# Product Implications

Every interaction should improve the user's internal model.

This changes how we think about product design.

Instead of asking:

"What information should we present?"

We ask:

"What change should occur inside the user's mental model?"

That becomes the objective.

---

# AI Implications

Artificial Intelligence should update internal models.

Not simply generate information.

Successful AI should:

- identify patterns
- explain relationships
- reduce ambiguity
- communicate uncertainty
- improve confidence
- reconcile conflicting evidence

The output of AI is not text.

The output is a better internal model.

---

# Design Implications

Good design reduces the gap between:

Reality

↓

Representation

↓

Internal Model

The interface succeeds when the user's understanding becomes more accurate with less cognitive effort.

---

# Relationship To Other Models

Reality exists.

Representations model reality.

Internal models interpret representations.

Understanding emerges from improved internal models.

Decisions arise from understanding.

Actions change reality.

```
Reality
        │
        ▼
Representation
        │
        ▼
Internal Model
        │
        ▼
Understanding
        │
        ▼
Decision
        │
        ▼
Action
        │
        ▼
Reality
```

This loop represents the fundamental learning cycle of the Identity Operating System.

---

# Evaluation

When evaluating any feature ask:

Does this improve the user's internal model?

Does it reduce misconceptions?

Does it organize complexity?

Does it reduce unnecessary cognitive effort?

Does it improve future decision making?

---

# Open Questions

# Why This Matters

The purpose of an Identity Intelligence Platform is not to expose more information.

Its purpose is to improve the user's internal model of another person's identity.

This subtle distinction changes the objective of the entire company.

Search becomes model refinement.

Reports become model updates.

AI becomes model synchronization.

Monitoring becomes continuous model maintenance.

Identity Intelligence becomes the practice of helping people maintain increasingly accurate models of reality.
