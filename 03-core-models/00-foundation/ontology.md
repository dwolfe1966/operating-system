# Applied Ontology

> "Before we can build software, we must first decide what exists."

---

# Purpose

The Identity Operating System is built upon an ontology.

This ontology defines the fundamental concepts that exist within our universe and the relationships between them.

Everything else—philosophy, product, design, AI, engineering, and implementation—is derived from this model.

This document intentionally sits above implementation.

Its purpose is not to describe software.

Its purpose is to describe reality as it relates to Identity Intelligence.

---

# Why Ontology?

Every complex system is built upon assumptions.

Most organizations leave these assumptions implicit.

We choose to make them explicit.

Doing so creates:

- shared language
- shared mental models
- coherent architecture
- consistent design
- better AI
- better decisions

---

# Two Worlds

The Identity Operating System models two distinct worlds.

## Reality

Reality exists independently of observation.

People.

Organizations.

Events.

Relationships.

Time.

Reality is what actually exists.

---

## Cognition

People never interact directly with reality.

They construct internal models of reality.

Everything we understand is mediated through cognition.

The purpose of the product is to improve cognition.

---

# The Bridge

Reality and cognition are connected through representations.

Representations are the bridge between the world and the mind.

Maps.

Language.

Graphs.

Identity reports.

AI summaries.

Dashboards.

Timelines.

Every interface we build is a representation.

# Core Concepts

## Reality

Reality exists independently of observation.

Our software never changes reality directly.

It changes people's understanding of reality.

---

## Objects

Objects are things that exist.

Examples include:

- people
- organizations
- identities
- events
- evidence

Objects possess properties.

Objects participate in relationships.

---

## Evidence

Evidence supports or weakens claims about reality.

Evidence does not create truth.

It improves confidence.

---

## Confidence

Confidence estimates the reliability of our current model.

Confidence is not certainty.

Confidence should always be explicit.

---

## Representation

Representations organize reality so that humans can reason about it.

The quality of a representation determines the quality of understanding.

---

## Internal Model

People construct internal models of reality.

Software does not directly create understanding.

It improves the user's internal model.

---

## Understanding

Understanding is the refinement of a person's internal model.

Understanding is the primary objective of the product.

---

## Decision

People ultimately use understanding to make decisions.

Better understanding should lead to better decisions.

---

## Action

Actions influence reality.

The loop then begins again.

---

# Guiding Principle

The Identity Operating System exists to reduce the gap between:

Reality

and

A person's internal model of reality.

Everything we build should contribute to reducing that gap.

---

# Evaluation

Every concept added to this ontology should satisfy four criteria.

## Explanatory Power

Does it explain many downstream decisions?

---

## Predictive Power

Does it help us anticipate behavior?

---

## Transferability

Will it remain useful independent of implementation?

---

## Actionability

Can designers, engineers, AI systems, and product managers use it to make better decisions?

---

# Current Working Questions

Several important questions remain unresolved.

## Is Identity an object or a representation?

Current thinking:

Identity is an object whose purpose is to represent a person.

Further work required.

---

## Is Trust part of cognition or a property?

Current thinking:

Trust exists within cognition.

Confidence exists within representations.

---

## Where does Time belong?

Time may not be an object.

Time may be a dimension that influences every object and process.

---

## How should AI fit into the ontology?

Current thinking:

AI is not another object.

AI is an engine that improves representations.

---

# Editorial Principle

This ontology is intentionally incomplete.

Its purpose is not to establish permanent truth.

Its purpose is to provide our current best model for reasoning about Identity Intelligence.

As our understanding improves, this ontology should evolve.

Evolution is expected.

Coherence is required.
