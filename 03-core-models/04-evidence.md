# Evidence

> "Identity is not discovered.
>
> Identity is inferred from evidence."

---

# Purpose

This document defines our current model of Evidence.

Evidence is the atomic unit of the Identity Operating System.

Everything we know about an identity originates from evidence.

Without evidence there are no representations.

Without representations there is no understanding.

Without understanding there are no decisions.

---

# Core Question

What is evidence?

---

# Working Model

Evidence is an observable fact that increases or decreases confidence in a hypothesis about reality.

Evidence is not truth.

Evidence supports or weakens possible explanations of reality.

Evidence accumulates.

Evidence conflicts.

Evidence changes.

Evidence expires.

Evidence is interpreted.

---

# Why This Model Exists

Traditional people-search products treat public records as the product.

Identity Intelligence treats public records as evidence.

This distinction changes everything.

Addresses are evidence.

Phone numbers are evidence.

Property ownership is evidence.

Court filings are evidence.

Employment is evidence.

Social profiles are evidence.

Every observation contributes evidence toward one or more hypotheses.

---

# Evidence Is Atomic

Evidence should not be decomposed into "records."

Records are storage constructs.

Evidence is an epistemological construct.

The platform should reason about evidence rather than databases.

---

# Evidence Supports Hypotheses

Example:

Observation:

123 Main Street appears in a county property record.

Evidence:

This person may have lived at 123 Main Street.

Hypothesis:

These two records refer to the same individual.

Confidence:

Medium

The important insight:

The address itself is not the identity.

It is evidence supporting an identity model.

---

# Evidence Is Never Perfect

Evidence may be:

Complete

Incomplete

Contradictory

Ambiguous

Outdated

Incorrect

Missing

Confidence should reflect these realities rather than hiding them.

---

# Product Implications

Search becomes evidence collection.

Identity becomes evidence synthesis.

AI becomes evidence interpretation.

Monitoring becomes evidence accumulation.

Reports become evidence representations.

Confidence becomes evidence quality.

---

# AI Implications

Artificial Intelligence should reason about evidence.

Tasks include:

- weighing evidence

- reconciling conflicting evidence

- identifying missing evidence

- estimating confidence

- explaining conclusions

- highlighting uncertainty

AI should expose its reasoning whenever appropriate.

---

# Design Implications

The interface should distinguish between:

Observation

Evidence

Inference

Conclusion

Users should understand not only what the product believes but why.

The reasoning process should become visible.

---

# Relationship To Identity

Identity should not be viewed as a static object.

Identity is our current best hypothesis supported by available evidence.

As evidence changes,

identity evolves.

This naturally supports:

- monitoring

- alerts

- timelines

- AI updates

- confidence revision

Identity becomes a living model rather than a static report.

---

# Relationship To Confidence

Confidence is not evidence.

Confidence is an evaluation of the quality, quantity and consistency of available evidence.

Evidence changes.

Confidence changes.

Identity changes.

---

# Evaluation

When evaluating evidence ask:

Is it observable?

Is it relevant?

Is it current?

Is it independent?

Does it strengthen or weaken a hypothesis?

Can it be explained?

Can conflicting evidence coexist?

The transition from public records to Identity Intelligence begins here.

Traditional products organize data.

The competitive advantage of the future is unlikely to come from owning more data.

Every future capability—

AI,

Identity Maps,

Monitoring,
